Omellete in bag

  • Time: 30mins
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
eggs 3
cherry tomatoes 4
cabbage, thinly sliced 1/8
ham slices 2
shredded cheese 30 grams
mushrooms 2
milk 2 tablespoons
mayonaise 3 tablespoons
salt 1/2 teaspoon
Pepper
Tomato Sauce

Step 1: Cut cherry tomatoes and ham into 1 centimeter cubes.
Tear basil leaves into small pieces by hand. Slice mushrooms thinly.

Step 2: Place cabbage and salt into a plastic bag and massage to soften the cabbage.

Step 3: Dump the rest of the ingredients into the bag and mix well with your hands.

Step 4: Making sure to get rid of the air inside, close the bag tightly.

Step 5: Boil some water in a saucepan.

Step 6: Place the bag inside and boil for 6 minutes. Turn over and continue boiling to make sure the egg has thoroughly cooked.

Step 7: Open the bag and slice the omelette onto a plate. Serve with tomato sauce if desired.

Gnocci Mac and Cheese

  • Time: 30mins
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients Quantity
gnocchi 1 pound
butter 2 tablespoons
flour 1/2 cup
mustard powder 1 teaspoon
cayenne pepper 1 teaspoon
Salt
Freshly Ground black pepper
milk 1 3/4 cups
Double Gloucester cheese 1/2 ounces
provolone 3 1/2 ounces
cheddar 3 1/2 ounces
bacon, crisped in oil 6 ounces
Parmesan 3 1/2 ounces
ricotta 3 1/2 ounces
Leafy herbs to garnish 6 ounces

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 390 degree.

Step 2: Add the gnocchi to a large pan of salted water and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.

Step 3: Start making the sauce by adding the butter to a medium pan on a medium heat.
When it's melted add the flour, mustard powder and cayenne pepper and stir together until well combined.
Turn the heat down and start adding the milk slowing, constantly stirring as you go. Once all the milk is added, add the grated cheeses (except Parmesan), and stir until the sauce starts to thicken.
Season to taste.

Step 4: Stir in the gnocchi and bacon and transfer to an ovenproof dish.
Sprinkle over the Parmesan, dollop over the ricotta and distribute the herb leaves.
Place in the oven for around 15 minutes until the Parmesan has melted, the sauce is bubbling and the leaves have crisped.

Herb and Mustard Sirlion With Baked Potatoes

  • Time: 30mins
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
small russet potatoes 4
sirloin steak 1 1/2 pounds
spicy mustard 2 tablespoons
crumbled herbes de Provence 2 teaspoons
unsalted butter 5 tablespoons
Kosher salt
Freshly Ground pepper
chopped fresh chives 2 tablespoons
chopped scallion greens 2 tablespoons

Step 1: Pierce the potatoes a few times with a fork,
then microwave until soft, about 15 minutes.

Step 2: Meanwhile, pierce both sides of the steak with a fork.
Mix the mustard and herbes de Provence in a bowl and rub all over the steak.

Step 3: Heat a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat.
Add 1 tablespoon butter; as soon as it melts, add the steak and sear on one side until browned, about 7 minutes.
Turn and brown the other side, about 4 more minutes for medium-rare.
Transfer the steak to a cutting board, season with salt and pepper, and top with 1 tablespoon butter.
Let rest at least 5 minutes.

Step 4: Return the skillet to medium-high heat.
Add the potatoes and turn to coat on all sides with the pan juices.
Cook until the skins are slightly crisp, about 3 minutes.

Step 5: Mix the remaining 3 tablespoons butter with the chives and season with salt.
Thinly slice the steak on the bias.
Serve with the potatoes, chive butter and mustard.
